## Lessons / process changes

### 1. Broker permissions are a precondition, not paperwork
IBKR options approval takes 1-2 business days. The UFO Disclosure Trade was sized for options from day one. When the catalyst hit on 2026-05-06, the trade was unexecutable. **Action: submit options-trading permission requests at IBKR (and any other broker routed through) before the next event-driven thesis goes live.** Treat broker approvals as part of trade-readiness setup, same level as wallet provisioning.

### 2. Pre-configure foreign-equity routes
SFTBY ADR is the lowest-friction US-listed expression of the SoftBank rerate. The thesis flagged this. The IBKR account needs JP-equity or ADR-trading enabled and tested **before** the next signal fires, so execution is one click. Same applies to any non-US name we end up writing into a thesis.

### 3. Currency-aware bankroll math
Chad's $1K cash was 1K **CAD**, not USD. At ~0.73 CAD/USD that's ~$730 USD. Trade plans denominated in USD must convert from the account's base currency before sizing. Add a CAD/USD line to the journal preamble going forward.

### 4. When narrative is already attaching pre-catalyst, don't fade it
$1K UFO compression plan picked AMTM ("safer / hasn't moved") over AMSC ("already moved 9.4% pre-market"). AMSC continued to +19.65% by close; AMTM didn't move. Lesson: a name running pre-catalyst is signaling that the narrative is attaching to that specific story. Anti-momentum-bias is wrong when the catalyst is hours away.

### 5. Hedged-spread vs unhedged-directional is a real choice
The AGTI Tracker overlay (long 9984 / short NIYM6) returned +4.6% gross — exactly what a SOTP-spread isolation should. The unhedged SFTBY equity returned +8.9%, almost double. The overlay isn't wrong, but it's solving for "isolate the spread" while the trade was directional. Decide which one you want before sizing.
