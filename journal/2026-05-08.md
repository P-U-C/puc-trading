## Process lessons added today

### 1. Announcement is the catalyst. Release is noise.

When a binary catalyst has a pre-announce stage (someone in authority says "the thing is coming on date X"), the announcement IS the event. The release itself is anti-climactic because the binary uncertainty was already removed.

**Why the structure works this way:** options pricing reflects implied probability times outcome size. Pre-announcement, there's a real distribution -- maybe 40% the release happens this week, 30% next week, 20% next month, 10% never. The announcement collapses that distribution to ~95% "happens on date X" -- which is most of the move. The release itself only adds the remaining 5% of probability mass.

**How to apply going forward:**
- For any event-driven thesis with a pre-announce structure, treat it as two distinct catalysts: announcement (big) and release (small).
- Exit windows are: 1) on the announcement-day strength, or 2) the morning after the announcement before sell-the-news drift starts.
- Specifically NOT: hold through to the release, expecting a second pop.

This is now codified for the AGTI report stream too. Many of the AGTI signals have a similar two-stage structure (e.g., "OPEC summit on date X", "election on date X"). The announcement of the date is the trade.

### 2. Conviction-weighted sizing beats equal-weight basket

The original UFO book was approximately 48% AMTM / 52% AMSC. After the fact, AMSC carried the entire book; AMTM was net negative. The thesis correctly identified AMSC as the alien-propulsion narrative play, but allocated only marginal extra weight to it.

**How to apply:** when a basket has a clear single-leg conviction winner (the leg you'd trade if you could only pick one), size that leg at 70-80% of book minimum. The other legs are diversification only when they have *independent* upside; AMTM and AMSC were both betting on the same UFOD-flow thesis, so they weren't actually diversified.

This is also a lesson that connects back to the 5/7 journal entry on hedged-vs-unhedged. The pattern: when expressions in a "basket" share a single underlying thesis, picking the cleanest expression at full size beats spraying across all of them.

### 3. The IBKR delay cost ~$1,200 on the original book

Options permission approved today, 5/8. Trade peaked 5/6. Two-day approval window cost the full announcement-day rip.

This was already on the lessons list from yesterday's journal, but quantified now. **The cost of broker permission paperwork is real and trade-specific** -- it's not a fixed admin cost, it's the difference between catching the trade and missing it.

**How to apply:** all event-driven setups now require a pre-trade checklist that includes:
- [ ] All required broker permissions verified (Options approved, Pink Sheets / OTC if needed, Futures if needed)
- [ ] Account base currency confirmed in USD-equivalent terms
- [ ] Foreign-equity routes pre-tested if the trade names a non-US instrument
- [ ] Catalyst date(s) flagged with both announcement-stage and release-stage exits pre-decided
