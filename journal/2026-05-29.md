# Trade Diary -- May 29, 2026

## Theme of the day

**The screen was manufacturing its own edge.** The convergence/mispricing paper
book was down ~32% and it wasn't a bad thesis — it was the engine misapplying a
good one. The lesson: *a mispricing ratio computed against the wrong tenor is not
an edge, it's an artifact.* Same day, fixed it, and made the screen direction-aware
so it stops buying calls on a short thesis.

---

## What actually happened (the −32% book)

Every one of the 13 open mispricing positions was the **same bet wearing 13
tickets**: theme `cicadas`, catalyst `cat_noaa_enso_2026_06` (the 2026-06-11 NOAA
ENSO print), four *correlated* ag ETFs (WEAT/CORN/DBA/FXA), all call spreads, **all
expiring 2026-06-18** — one week after the print. $1,918 deployed, marked −$609.

Two root causes in the engine, both now patched:

| Bug | Was | Fix |
|-----|-----|-----|
| Tenor | `MIN_POST_EVENT_BUFFER_DAYS = 5` → bought the monthly expiry 1 week after the catalyst | raised to **45** → reaches the Aug–Jan tenors the chain already carried (matches `trades/cicadas.md` "Jul'26–Jan'27") |
| Concentration | per-*ticker* cap only (5%); blind to correlation | added per-**theme** (10%) + per-**catalyst** (8%) caps + **max 2 tickers/catalyst** |
| Direction | assumed every signal bullish → bought calls | calendar now carries `theme_directions`; screen buys **puts for short legs** |

## The headline finding

When I re-ran the *corrected* engine on the same names at the *proper* tenor, the
mispricing **mostly evaporated**:

- FXA ratio 3.8–6.6 → **1.71** (still real)
- CORN 2.2–2.6 → **1.23** (fair, no edge)
- WEAT/DBA 1.8–2.4 → **0.82/0.83** (fair)
- MOO/SOYB/SB → **over**priced

Why: edge = `thesis_implied_move ÷ market_implied_move`. At a 1-week expiry the
market-implied move is tiny, so a multi-month thesis looks like a 2–6× mispricing.
Stretch the expiry to where the move has time to play out and the market-implied
move grows — **the "mispricing" was the short tenor, not underpricing.** Done
correctly, this whole book is one small FXA spread, not $1,918 across 13 legs.

## The direction lesson (sharper version of the FXA story)

The one trade that survived the tenor fix — FXA — was **bullish calls**. But
`cicadas.md`'s thesis is **short AUD/USD** (Australia dryness). The screen had no
direction model: it assumed every "underpriced move" was upside. So it kept the
one genuine mispricing *with the wrong sign*. The bullish FXA call even showed
+15.7% — **precisely because AUD has risen since 5/19, i.e. the thesis has been
wrong so far.** A screen that flatters a losing thesis is worse than no screen.

After wiring direction in, the book re-screens as an FXA **put** spread (short
AUD), correctly **−22.2%**. That number is the truth; the +15.7% was a lie of sign.

## Process lessons added today

### 1. A mispricing ratio is only as honest as its tenor.
Never compare a multi-stage / multi-month thesis move against the implied move of
a near-dated option. Either match the option tenor to the thesis horizon, or
don't claim an edge. Codified as the 45-day post-event tenor floor.

### 2. Per-ticker risk caps don't see correlated bets.
Four ag ETFs on one weather catalyst each passed the 5% per-ticker cap and
collectively became 100% of the book. Concentration must be capped at the level
the *thesis* lives (theme/catalyst), not just the instrument.

### 3. A directionless screen will buy the wrong side of a correct thesis.
"Underpriced move" ≠ "buy calls." Direction is a thesis input, now sourced from
`calendar/catalysts.yaml: theme_directions` (default long, so nothing else
changed). Put-spread pricing added to `remark.py` so bearish marks move the right
way.

### 4. Hit rate without average return is not an edge (AGTI).
AGTI cleared its go-live gate on count + 57% hit rate, but average return is ~flat
— the edge is entirely equity-longs (62–63%); shorts/ETFs/forex are 38–44% drag,
the same macro-proxy failure the backtest flagged. Shipped `EQUITY_LONG_ONLY` so
the cron only deploys the proven class. (Also fixed an 11-error fill bug: the
weekday-only calendar tried to fill on Memorial Day; `fetch_open` now rolls
forward to the next session.)
