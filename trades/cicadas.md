## TL;DR

The market is not underpricing "El Niño exists." That is now obvious.

The market may still be underpricing the **joint distribution**:

1. El Niño becomes strong enough to matter.
2. Fertilizer remains expensive into the 2027 planting cycle.
3. One or two key crop regions fail at the same time.
4. Policy reflex amplifies — export restrictions, reserve buying, panic imports.
5. Implied vol reprices only after physical damage becomes visible.

The trade is **not** "food goes up." The trade is **supply elasticity collapses at the exact moment the market discovers that fertilizer, weather, and policy are the same trade.**

Per-sleeve cap: 6% NAV peak deployment. Halve at -3% sleeve drawdown. Stage entries in thirds keyed to **physical-coupling confirmation**, not headline.

## Indicator hierarchy (in order of importance)

ENSO forecasts usually fail when people overreact to surface warmth before the atmosphere commits. Trade off the upstream physics, not the public scoreboard.

1. **WWV / upper-ocean heat content** — best oceanic precursor (leads Niño3.4 by 6-9 months per NOAA PMEL).
2. **D20 (20°C isotherm depth)** — direct thermocline proxy. Watch east of the dateline.
3. **WWBs (westerly wind bursts)** — needed for stronger development. Repeated through Jun-Aug 2026 is the bullish path.
4. **MJO phase + amplitude** — helps trigger WWBs. Watch for equatorial convective envelope.
5. **Kelvin wave propagation** — converts wind forcing into eastern-Pacific warm anomalies. ~45 day basin-crossing time.
6. **Weekly Niño-3.4 SST** — public scoreboard. Last, not first.
7. **IRI / ECMWF plume median + spread** — scenario sizing input only.
8. **BOM Australia rainfall + JAMSTEC IOD** — cleanest direct crop signal.
9. **IMD India monsoon progression** — fastest macro-food transmission.
10. **South America planting weather verification** — determines soy/corn sign from Sep onward.

## Why this isn't a trend-corpus theme

Sector themes in `trend-corpus/trends/` model sustained sector dynamics
that compound over years (peptides, AI infra, nuclear). This is a
time-bound macro thesis with a verification window of weeks and an
expiry of about a year. It traded against existing instruments, not
against a sector-curated entity universe.

The catalyst calendar entries live in `calendar/catalysts.yaml` so the
mispricing screen picks them up automatically. When the trade goes
live, the sleeve mirror lives under `paper-journal/cicadas/` with
the same shape as `paper-journal/agti/` and `paper-journal/mispricing/`.
